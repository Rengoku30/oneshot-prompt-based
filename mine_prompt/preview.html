The discipline

Four convictions,
held without exception.

We do not have a house style. We have a house discipline — a small set of beliefs we apply to every commission, regardless of size, budget, or fashion. These four are the spine of every building we have made.

01

Light

We begin with the path of the sun. Orientation, aperture, and shadow are decided before a single line is drawn for form.

02

Material

Concrete, stone, oak, plaster. Few materials, chosen once, allowed to age without apology. Honesty over novelty.

03

Restraint

Every element must earn its place. We remove until only the necessary remains — then remove again, if it can bear it.

04

Permanence

A building is a long sentence spoken slowly. We design for the fourth generation, not the first photograph.

Process

Four movements,
played slowly.

Architecture is not a sprint to a render. It is a sequence of patient, distinct phases — each given the time it demands. We do not skip steps, and we do not rush the slow ones.

IWeeks 1–4

Listening

Before drawings, we read the site. We walk it at dawn and dusk, in rain and clear weather, and listen to the people who will inhabit it.

IIWeeks 4–12

Reduction

The brief is interrogated until only its spine remains. We sketch by hand, in pencil, until the idea will not simplify further.

IIIMonths 3–9

Drawing

Precise drawings and full-scale mock-ups. Materials sampled against the actual light of the site. Nothing left to interpretation.

IVYear 1+

Building

We remain on site through construction. The architect who drew the detail is the architect who watches it built.
